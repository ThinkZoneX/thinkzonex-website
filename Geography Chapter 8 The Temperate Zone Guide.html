üèïÔ∏è 10. TRIBES AND NOMADIC HERDING

What is Nomadic Herding?

Moving from one place to another with herds of animals in search of pastures (grazing land) to feed the cattle.

Practiced in: Arid (dry) margins of temperate areas in the south

Major Tribes

Kyrgyz Tribe
  • Location: European Steppes
  • Animals: Horses, Sheep, Goats
  • Lifestyle: Nomadic herders
Native Americans (Indians)
  • Location: North America (Prairies)
  • Traditional: Nomadic herders
  • Today: Settled life
Khoikhois
  • Location: Africa (Veld)
  • Traditional: Nomadic herders
  • Today: Settled life

‚ö° Changes in Recent Decades

FROM: Nomadic herders (moving with animals)
TO: Settled life (living in one place)
NEW OCCUPATIONS: Cultivation and Mining
STATUS: Part of non-tribal society

üìñ 11. IMPORTANT VOCABULARY

Arid: Very dry

Grasslands: Vast open areas covered with different varieties of grass

Pampas: Grassland in South America meaning "plain"

Prairie: Grassland in North America meaning "meadow"

Steppe: A large, flat area of land without trees in south-eastern Europe or Asia

Veld: Grassland in South Africa, which means "a field"

Continental Climate: Climate in interior of continents with warm summers and very cold winters

Maritime/Oceanic Climate: Climate near oceans with warm summers and mild winters

Extensive Agriculture: Large-scale farming using scientific methods and modern machinery

Nomadic Herding: Moving from place to place with animal herds in search of pastures
